History of the Mouloudia Club of Algiers

Club history In the year 1921 a group of young Algerians from the Casbah (Historic district of Algiers) came into contact with their compatriots from Bab El Oued (Popular Quarter in Algiers), with the intention of founding an Algerian football club. Contact was established largely thanks to Aouf Hamoud, who acted as liaison between the two neighborhoods. The meeting which gave birth to the club took place in the back shop of the "benachere" café, currently "Yahi". Because this day (August 7, 1921) coincided with the "Mouloud", the club was called "Mouloudia Club of Algiers". As for the colors (green and red) they were chosen for these reasons: * Green: the sign of hope of the Algerian people and symbolic color of Islam. * Red: the sign of love of the homeland and sacrifice for it. Mouloudia is much more than just a football club. It was the only Algerian Muslim club. It subsequently provoked a real Muslim sports movement, with its popularity which continued to grow, to the point of worrying the French colonialist regime. His first season, in 1921/1922, in "ANAFA", had its ups and downs. The following season, the MC. Algiers was officially admitted to the Algerian regional league (FFF), in the 4th division. But despite these great performances and the quality of his game the MC. Algiers was unable to access the higher division, under the shadow of the French press which avoided publishing the results and news of the club, for fear of increasing its popularity of the club, but also by the appearance of the “BORDES” law which required Muslim clubs to contain at least two European players, with the intention of reducing the popularity of the ever-growing club. So we had to wait 14 seasons to see the MC. Algiers accession to the Legion of Honor. This was done during the 1935/1936 season, after total domination by the MC. Algiers during the year, which ended with a play-off match against the French club "Olympique Maringo", which was replayed 3 times after 2 draws, which was a first in the history of the (FFF). But at the end of the 3rd meeting it was the MC. Algiers who won with a score of 2-1. It was then the first climb in the history of the club. During his first season in the Legion of Honor, the MC. Algiers was in first place in the ranking, but saw accession always elude it during the last day of the championship, which earned it the nickname by the French press of: "The eternal dauphin". The good ranking of the MC. Algiers in this division as well as its good performance allowed it to play THE NORTH AFRICAN CUP. But the latter did not go beyond the 8th finals. The MC. Algiers also played some friendly matches against: Lokomotiv Moscow, Rapid Vienna, Olympique de Nice, ... During the 1939/1940 season, nicknamed "the season of war" due to the 2nd World War, and for the same cause, the French championship experienced a delay as well as the sharing of the division of honor in Algeria into 3 groups. The MC. Algiers had dominated in its group, then beat the leaders of groups A and B to find themselves champion of "the season of war", which put its many supporters in a euphoric state in the streets of Algiers. If the title won by the MC. Algiers in 1939/1940 was only symbolic (friendly tournament), at the end of the 1943/1944 season it was able to finish as leader and win the title of champion of the Algerian league, ahead of ASSE. This title was the last before national liberation. Before the cessation of any Algerian club, and the abstention from any participation in the various competitions, by responding to the call of the FLN in 1956, there was a certain match which remains engraved in the history of Algerian football . This match took place between the MC. Algiers and the JS. Kabylie as part of the "North African Cup" and which ended with an overwhelming victory for the MC. Algiers by 7-0. This match took place on October 4, 1951. After national liberation (1962), the MC. Algiers as well as the USM. Algiers had qualified to play in the final of the regional championship, but the MC. Algiers lost it by a score of 3-0. One year later (1964) the MC. Algiers was able to do no better than secure a place among the clubs which will play the first national championship in 1964/1965. During this season the Minister of Youth and Sports relegated the MC. Algiers in D2 where he remained for 3 seasons.
